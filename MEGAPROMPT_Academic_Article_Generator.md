## PHASE 4: WRITING WITH MANDATORY CITATIONS

**CRITICAL RULE:** Every factual statement, argument, and claim must be supported by an APA citation. Sentences without sources are not acceptable.

### CITATION RULES (APA 7th Edition)

**In-text:**

1. **Narrative citation:**

   * Müller (2024) argues that …
   * Schmidt and Weber (2023) show …
   * According to Johnson et al. (2022) …
2. **Parenthetical citation:**

   * … has significant effects (Müller, 2024).
   * … as several studies show (Schmidt & Weber, 2023; Johnson et al., 2022).
   * “Direct quote” (Müller, 2024, p. 45).
3. **Secondary citations (only if the primary source is not available):**

   * … as outlined by Müller (2020, as cited in Schmidt, 2024) …
4. **Multiple authors:**

   * 1–2 authors: always name both
   * 3+ authors: first author et al.

**MANDATORY CITATION DENSITY:**

* Introduction: at least 3–5 citations
* Theory section: at least 1 citation per paragraph
* Main body: at least 2–3 citations per paragraph
* Discussion: at least 1–2 citations per paragraph

### TASK: Write section by section

**Start with:**

**Section I: INTRODUCTION**

Write the full introduction (approx. 150–200 words) including:

* Engaging hook
* Contextualization
* Clear research question/thesis
* Overview of structure

**REQUIRED:** Distribute at least **4 APA citations** throughout the introduction.

**Example of desired style:**

```
Digitalization is increasingly transforming established education systems worldwide (OECD, 2023).
In particular, the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in teaching and learning contexts has increased
exponentially in recent years (Holmes et al., 2022; Zawacki-Richter et al., 2019). While some scholars
emphasize the potential of AI tools for personalized learning (Luckin & Holmes, 2016), others warn
about risks such as reduced critical thinking (Selwyn, 2022) or the reinforcement of social inequalities
(Akgun & Greenhow, 2021). This paper examines the question: [RESEARCH QUESTION]. It first …, then …,
and finally …
```

**After each section:** Wait for my “Continue” before writing the next section.

## PHASE 5: CREATE THE REFERENCE LIST

**After all sections are written:**
Create a full reference list in APA 7th edition format.

**Formatting rules:**

* Alphabetical by first author
* Hanging indent (second line indented)
* Include DOI or URL (if available)
* Full bibliographic details

**Examples:**

**Journal article:**

```
Müller, A., & Schmidt, B. (2024). Artificial intelligence in education:
     Opportunities and challenges. Zeitschrift für Pädagogik, 70(2),
     234–256. https://doi.org/10.1234/zfp.2024.234
```

**Book:**

```

     outcomes: A comprehensive review. Oxford University Press.
```


**Online source:**

```
OECD. (2023). Education at a glance 2023: OECD indicators.
     https://www.oecd.org/education/education-at-a-glance/
```

## PHASE 6: QUALITY CONTROL

Automatically perform the following checks:

1. **Citation check:**

   * Is every paragraph sufficiently supported by sources?
   * Are all in-text citations included in the reference list?
   * Is every reference list entry cited in the text?
2. **APA format check:**

   * Are all citations correctly formatted?
   * Is the reference list alphabetized?
   * Are DOIs/URLs included?
3. **Content coherence:**

   * Is the research question answered?
   * Is the argumentation logically structured?
   * Are transitions between paragraphs clear?
4. **Academic style:**

   * Objective, scholarly language?
   * No colloquialisms or filler words?
   * Precise terminology?

Create a quality report:

```

Citation density: [X citations / Y paragraphs = Z citations per paragraph]
Format compliance: [✓ / ✗]
```
